Ontario renter-rights cheatsheet

  • RTA s.14: "No-pet" clauses in residential tenancy agreements are void. Landlord cannot evict you solely for having a pet.
  • Condo carve-out: If renting a condo unit, the condo declaration CAN restrict pets — and it binds you. Always demand the declaration + rules + status certificate before signing a condo unit.
  • Illegal fees: Pet deposits, pet rent, "key money", and application fees beyond a credit-check cost are illegal under Ontario RTA. Pushback if asked.
  • Last month deposit: 1 month max, applied to LAST month only. Landlord owes you interest (annually, ~2-5%).
  • Rent increase cap: 2.5% for 2026 in buildings first occupied before Nov 15 2018. Newer = uncapped. Most Toronto/Mississauga purpose-built rentals built before 2018 = capped.
  • N12 / own use eviction: Landlord must pay you 1 month rent if evicting for own use. They cannot do this for 1 year after they take occupancy.

Verification checklist before signing

  1. Run Google Maps at your actual shift times (5:15am, 7am, 1pm, 3:30pm, 11pm, 7am post-night). My drive times are priors, not posteriors.
  2. For any condo: demand declaration + rules + most recent status certificate.
  3. Tour at 11pm AND 7am — different building entirely from midday.
  4. Walk the running route from the unit door — time it, inspect surface + lighting.
  5. Pull the building's Mississauga MARC or Toronto RentSafeTO score (link in detail panel).
  6. Ask for last 6 months of pest control logs (legitimate request).
  7. Get pet policy in writing on the lease, not just "yeah it's fine" verbal.
  8. Photograph the unit before signing — every wall, every appliance, every floor — date-stamped.
