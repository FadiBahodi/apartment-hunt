# 🏘️ LIBERTY VILLAGE (6 units)

## Area-level reality

Liberty Village is the **single best risk-adjusted area** for an ER attending at CVH. Here's why:

**Geography wins:** Liberty sits between Strachan (east exit) and Dufferin (west exit). Both feed directly south to Gardiner westbound. AM commutes from any Liberty building are 39-40 minutes — tighter than anywhere else on this list. Fri 5pm worst case stays at ~50 min because you're heading WITH the rush, not against it.

**The block-by-block:** East Liberty Street is the spine. The "tri-tower" complex (65, 75, 85 East Liberty by Plaza, 2013) sits at the eastern end near Strachan — best Strachan exit for Gardiner. Western Battery is north-central, closer to King St (better for nightlife, slightly less direct to Gardiner). Lynn Williams is the quietest residential street, dead-ends at the park. Joe Shuster Way is southwest — the Urbancorp/Fuzion mess, avoid.

**Run options:** Liberty Village Striders meets Wed 6am at Aroma Espresso (120 Lynn Williams). The route runs the King-Liberty-pedestrian-bridge → Lake Shore Blvd → Martin Goodman Trail. **For a 100km/wk runner, this is the best run-club geography of any neighborhood on this list.** F45 Liberty Village is the gym anchor. Metro at 137 East Liberty is the grocery.

**Nightlife:** Liberty Village ITSELF is a quieter eat-and-drink scene (LCBO + Birch + Brazen Head). For real King W bars you walk 8-10 min east to King+Strachan (Strachan King Street strip starts there). Ossington = 18-22 min walk depending on which Liberty building.

**Vibe & dating demographic:** Liberty Village is 28-35 young professionals. NOT a "geezer" area at all. Sunday Drake Hotel patios + Wednesday CrossFit + Saturday Park Days. Sweet spot for a 27yo ER attending who wants social proximity without the chaos of King W proper.
